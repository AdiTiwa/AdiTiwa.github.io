Genetic Ansatz Construction

Using typical measures of quantum circuits, our paper constructs and trains a reinforcement learning-based heuristic function to optimize using a genetic algorithm. In optimization, the algorithm constructs optimal circuits to use as ansatz for Variational Quantum Eigensolvers.

Tree-of-Thought Agreement/Cognitive Dissonance in LLMs

In models that corroborate the outputs of Large Language Models, possible biases in system prompts can lead to inaccuracies in generated answers. In order to diagnose and address "cognitive dissonance", we explore by prompting language models to "debate" over a generated tree of thought, and measure convergence on agreed solutions.

Python Language Models Machine Learning

Hidden Subgroup Problem for Solvable Symmetric Groups

The Hidden Subgroup Problem is the problem (with respect to reduction) that underlies most cryptographic algorithms. This project inspects this problem over a specific type of group, namely the simplest non-abelian (non-commutative) structures: Symmetric Groups. We specific inspect the collection of groups that have a sub-normal/derived sequence, called solvable groups, due to observed potential in other abelian groups.

We rely on intuition from psychology to store experiences in environments to stimulate success and estimations, and find significant improvements when applying to common open-worlds like Minecraft. Ant Colony Optimization for LLM Tree of Thoughts

Modern reasoning language models utilize intermediate reasoning steps to generate accurate solutions to difficult problems, but often can make mistakes through their reasoning chain after many iterations. This paper presents a method that strengthens exploration pathways for miniature expert "ant" LLMs to come to a consensus on optimal reasoning pathways in difficult problems.

Post-Quantum Encryption Library

Due to advances in Quantum Algorithms, such as Peter Shor's factoring algorithm, many modern encryption algorithms are under scrutiny. While hardware lags behind algorithm development, the possibility of cyber-attacks prompts the development of post-quantum encryption methods. With Doug Strain at Google's Quantum AI, we developed an example library for popular methods like Crystals-KYBER, as well as toy algorithms to be broken by quantum computers.
